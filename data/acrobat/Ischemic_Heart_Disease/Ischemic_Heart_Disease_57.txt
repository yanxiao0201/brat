A four-member family was admitted to the local hospital with complaints of abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea 5 h after consumption of wild mushrooms purchased from the local bazaar. Kidney and liver function tests were normal on admission. The family members were kept under observation in the emergency department with a diagnosis of mushroom poisoning. The type of wild mushroom ingested could not be established. Intravenous fluid was started and gastric lavage was performed immediately. The patients were treated with activated charcoal, N-acetyl cysteine (NAC), penicillin G, and silibin, which helped to reduce the absorption of the toxin through the intestines and the risk of hepatic toxicity.
